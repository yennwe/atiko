Chapter 8: The Final Hurt to Gu Shisheng

Lu Siyi took two days off from work to accompany Gu Shisheng. If it weren’t for Gu Shisheng insisting that Lu Siyi go back to work on the third day, Lu Siyi would have brought his work to the hospital.

“I need to take care of myself even when you’re not around. Qin Auntie will bring lunch at noon. Remember to video call me when you’re eating. I want to see you eat well.”

“Alright, you can relax. I’m not a child.”

Lu Siyi chuckled, reaching out to tap Gu Shisheng’s forehead. “When you’re with me, you’re always my baby.”

“Don’t…” Gu Shisheng’s cheeks reddened slightly. His pale, porcelain-like complexion had a hint of blush, delicate like a canary in a cage, fragile and in need of tender care.

Lu Siyi loved Gu Shisheng’s appearance. After ten years, the beauty and attractiveness had become a silent part of their lives, devoid of the initial stunning and thrilling sensation.

He lowered his head to kiss Gu Shisheng’s eyelashes, feeling the slight tremble beneath him. He helplessly spoke, “Why are you still so easily embarrassed?”

“You should go now, or you’ll be late.”

After seeing off Lu Siyi, Fu Cheng, who had been a light bulb for a long time, finally had a chance to speak.

“Now I have to admire you. It’s a love that others envy when they see it.” Fu Cheng sat beside him, sighing. Today, he was dressed in normal clothes, looking like a high-profile agent.

Indeed, for ten years, Lu Siyi loved Gu Shisheng the most. Few people could match Lu Siyi’s consistent, unwavering affection for his wife, treating him with the same indulgence as he did ten years ago.

In high school, Gu Shisheng met Lu Siyi and Fu Cheng.

The former became his final lover, and the latter became his best friend.

Gu Shisheng lowered his head, fingering his own fingers, a habit formed since childhood. When his depression erupted, he couldn’t control the impulse to self-harm.

He looked up at Fu Cheng. “But I envy you more. You live freely, without hesitation.”

Those who had wronged Fu Cheng in the past had been boycotted by him, leading to unemployment and bankruptcy. Now, he was still subject to public criticism.

“I want to take those photos, confront him, go hysterical with him, make a scene, go crazy with him, drag him down. No one should have it better than him.”

“But you see, I can’t.” He opened his hands, revealing the marks already scratched by his nails.

“He loves me so much. Everyone knows he loves me, including myself. He has sacrificed so much for me, but what have I done for him? I’ve done nothing for him and still hurt him, knowing that he loves me.”

Fu Cheng hugged Gu Shisheng. “Sheng Sheng, if it’s not working, let go. You still have me in the future.”

Gu Shisheng grinned, pure in his laughter. “Wait until I find the answer to the problem, then say goodbye to him. Don’t worry; I’m fine. Back when I chose to live, I told myself to treat myself well.”

He had accepted this situation a long time ago. From the first discovery, through countless later discoveries, entangled in whether Lu Siyi had changed or not, he began to let go.

Lu Siyi had indeed done what others dared not do to Gu Shisheng, but in the end, Lu Siyi became the one who hurt Gu Shisheng the most.

Loving and falling for Lu Siyi took too much effort. He didn’t even want to look, didn’t want to care.

Gu Shisheng, beyond the age of self-pity, gained maturity. After being discharged the next day, he went back to work at the gallery.

After repairing a whole painting, Gu Shisheng returned to his office and was amazed. On the desk were placed gardenia flowers emitting a faint fragrance, delicate blossoms swaying with the wind from the window.

The appearance of the youth caught him off guard.

Nanoha Grey first appeared in front of him, followed by the sweet and sour taste of candy being put into his mouth.

A flavor that filled his entire mouth.

The youth’s eyes were bright, looking at Gu Shisheng expectantly. “Brother Gu, is it delicious? Is it sweet? It’s citrus-flavored.”
